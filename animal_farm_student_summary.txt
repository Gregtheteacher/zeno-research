Animal Farm by George Orwell — Student Reading Summary
(Written as rough recall, not a scholarly summary)

The story is about a farm in England called Manor Farm. The farmer is Mr. Jones and he's mean and neglectful — he forgets to feed the animals sometimes and drinks too much. There's an old pig called Old Major who has a dream about animals being free and not being controlled by humans. He gathers all the animals together one night and tells them about his dream and teaches them a song called Beasts of England. He dies a few days later but the other animals remember what he said.

The pigs are the smartest animals and two of them — Napoleon and Snowball — start organizing the other animals. One night Mr. Jones forgets to feed them again and the animals just kind of revolt spontaneously. They chase Jones and the other humans off the farm and take it over. They rename it Animal Farm. The pigs paint seven commandments on the barn wall — rules about how animals should behave, like "four legs good, two legs bad" and "no animal shall kill another animal" and "all animals are equal."

At first things seem good. The animals work hard and the harvest is actually better than when Jones ran things. Snowball is trying to organize everything and he has all these ideas. He wants to build a windmill that would generate electricity and make their lives easier. Napoleon disagrees with basically everything Snowball proposes.

Then Napoleon does something really shocking — he has these dogs that he raised from puppies and he uses them to chase Snowball off the farm. After that Napoleon is basically in charge. He says they're not going to have debates anymore, the pigs will just make all the decisions. Squealer is another pig who is really good at talking and he explains everything Napoleon does and makes it sound reasonable even when it isn't.

Napoleon decides to build the windmill anyway even though it was Snowball's idea. He says Snowball was actually a traitor and enemy all along. The animals work really hard to build the windmill. Mr. Jones tries to come back and take the farm — this is called the Battle of the Cowshed — but the animals fight him off. Snowball was actually brave in that battle but later Napoleon rewrites history and says Snowball was a coward.

The pigs start changing the rules. The commandments on the barn wall get changed slightly when nobody is looking. Like it changes from "no animal shall drink alcohol" to "no animal shall drink alcohol to excess." The animals kind of remember things being different but Squealer convinces them they're misremembering.

Napoleon starts trading with humans which was supposed to be forbidden. He makes a deal with a farmer called Mr. Pilkington. Then there's a big moment where Napoleon accuses the hens and some other animals of being traitors working with Snowball and they get executed. This is really disturbing. The rule said no animal shall kill another animal but now animals are being killed.

Things get harder and harder. The animals are hungry and working more than they ever did under Jones. But Squealer always has statistics that show production is actually up. The sheep keep saying "four legs good, two legs bad" whenever anyone tries to think or question anything.

At the end the pigs are walking on two legs. They're wearing clothes. They're basically indistinguishable from humans. The last commandment gets changed to "all animals are equal but some animals are more equal than others." The other animals look through the farmhouse window and they can't tell the difference between the pigs and the humans anymore.

Main characters:
- Napoleon: the main pig, becomes the dictator, based on Stalin
- Snowball: the other main pig, has good ideas, gets chased away, based on Trotsky  
- Squealer: Napoleon's spokesperson, twists everything with words
- Boxer: the horse, works incredibly hard, loyal, his motto is "I will work harder" — gets sent to the knacker when he gets injured which is really sad
- Old Major: the original visionary pig who started everything, dies early
- Mr. Jones: the original farmer, drunk and neglectful
- The sheep: just repeat slogans, don't think for themselves
